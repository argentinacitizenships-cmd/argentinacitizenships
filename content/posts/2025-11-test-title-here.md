# Application Process

Although final administrative guidelines are pending, Argentina’s Citizenship by Investment process is expected to be streamlined and transparent:

⦁	**Pre-Application Assessment** – Preliminary review confirming eligibility under investment and background criteria.



⦁	Investment Commitment – Capital directed into a government-approved project supporting Argentina’s economic priorities.



⦁	Citizenship Submission – Formal application submitted to the Agency for Citizenship by Investment Programs, including investment proof and identity documents.



⦁	Due Diligence Review – Multi-agency background verification by the National Migration Authority, Financial Crimes Unit, and Intelligence Secretariat.



⦁	Approval & Passport Issuance – Under Decree 524/2025, applications must be decided within 30 business days, after which successful applicants can obtain full citizenship and an Argentine passport.





Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How much do I need to invest to qualify?

The minimum investment is expected to be around USD 500,000, directed toward approved productive sectors. Final confirmation will be published once the technical regulations are issued.

2. Do I need to live in Argentina to qualify?

No. The program is specifically designed to offer citizenship without residency, making it ideal for global investors seeking a second passport through Argentina investment immigration.

3. How long does the process take?

Under Decree 524/2025, authorities must issue a decision within 30 business days after submission, which makes it among the fastest timelines globally.

4. Can my family be included?

Yes. Spouses and dependent children are expected to qualify under a single family application.

5. When will applications open?

Applications are expected to open in late 2025 or early 2026, once final guidelines and eligible investment sectors are published.

6. Does Argentina allow dual citizenship?

Yes. Argentina recognizes and allows dual nationality, enabling applicants to keep their original passport.
