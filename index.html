A New Era of Opportunity: The Argentina Citizenship by Investment Explained

Argentina has officially entered the global investment immigration landscape with the launch of its Citizenship by Investment (CBI) program under Decree 524/2025.

For the first time, qualified investors can obtain Argentine citizenship without the traditional multi-year residency period, through a direct investment pathway.

Below, you'll find the most up-to-date verified details on how the program is being structured, and why Argentina's new CBI (also referred to as the Argentina Golden Visa) could quickly become one of the most sought-after options in the global citizenship market.

Why Argentina Introduced Its Citizenship by Investment Program

Argentina launched its Citizenship by Investment (CBI) program under Presidential Decree 524/2025 to attract qualified investors capable of driving national growth and innovation.

While final regulations are still pending, the program is expected to prioritize strategic investments in renewable energy, agribusiness, technology, tourism, and infrastructure development, key sectors essential to the country's long-term modernization and job creation.

In return, approved investors and their families will gain direct access to Argentine citizenship, bypassing the traditional residency route entirely.

Legal Basis & Program Overview

The Argentina Citizenship by Investment Program was formally established through Presidential Decree No. 524/2025, signed by President Javier Milei in July 2025. This decree amends the Nationality Law, enabling foreign investors to acquire citizenship through approved economic contributions without first establishing residency.

The program will be managed by the newly created Agency for Citizenship by Investment Programs within the Ministry of Economy, working in coordination with the National Migration Authority, Financial Crimes Unit, and Intelligence Secretariat to ensure compliance, transparency, and international due diligence standards.

Applications are expected to open in late 2025 or early 2026, once all technical and procedural regulations are finalized.

Key Benefits of Argentine Citizenship

Visa-Free Travel
The Argentine passport provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to over 172 countries, including the Schengen Area, United Kingdom, Ireland, Japan, and much of Asia. Argentina is also seeking to rejoin the U.S. Visa Waiver Program, which would allow citizens to enter the United States for up to 90 days without a visa.

Mercosur Freedom of Movement

Argentine citizens enjoy full freedom of movement and work across Mercosur member states, including Brazil, Uruguay, Paraguay, and associated nations, giving regional mobility and business opportunities.

U.S. E-2 Visa Eligibility

Argentine citizens are eligible for the U.S. E-2 Investor Visa, a highly sought-after option among entrepreneurs and investors who wish to live and operate businesses in the United States.

Dual Citizenship Permitted

Investors are not required to renounce their original nationality. Argentina fully recognizes dual citizenship, ensuring flexibility for global citizens.

Tax Considerations

Argentina applies taxation based on residency, not citizenship, meaning that citizens living abroad are generally not taxed on foreign-sourced income.

Fast Citizenship Pathway

Through this new Argentina Passport by Investment route, investors and their families can secure full citizenship within 6-12 months, eliminating years of traditional residency requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do I need to invest to qualify?

The minimum investment is expected to be around USD 500,000, directed toward approved productive sectors. Final confirmation will be published once the technical regulations are issued.

Do I need to live in Argentina to qualify?

No. The program is specifically designed to offer citizenship without residency, making it ideal for global investors seeking a second passport through Argentina investment immigration.

How long does the process take?

Under Decree 524/2025, authorities must issue a decision within 30 business days after submission, which makes it among the fastest timelines globally.

Can my family be included?

Yes. Spouses and dependent children are expected to qualify under a single family application.

When will applications open?

Applications are expected to open in late 2025 or early 2026, once final guidelines and eligible investment sectors are published.

Does Argentina allow dual citizenship?

Yes. Argentina recognizes and allows dual nationality, enabling applicants to keep their original passport.
